Student Activists For America

Manifest

Fri Jan 27, 2017 by SAFA.

WE BELIEVE

  1. We believe that engagement with human rights activism is the moral obligation of an American.
  2. We believe that students should be more engaged with their communities beyond their schools.
  3. We believe that ‘service learning quotas’ are objectionable reifications of this moral obligation to societal engagement and volunteerism.
  4. We believe in the power of small actions and their potential for big effects.
  5. We believe no person’s societal contributions should be limited by a lack of information
  6. We believe that encouraging students to assist with tasks in which they’re most competent increases their efficiency and engagement in those tasks
  7. We believe that a dissatisfaction with the status quo and want for change should be translated into action as quickly and effectively as possible, to reduce the risk of losing motivation.

WE WANT

  1. We want to enable students to efficiently find opportunities to improve the lives of their neighbors.
  2. We want to empower student activists to maximize the efficiency of their efforts by helping with things at which they are already skilled.
  3. We want to help students discover new areas in which to support their communities.
  4. We want to build a foundation of volunteerism, activism, and commitment to human rights that will enable students to be strong contributors to society for the rest of their life.
  5. We want to work with schools, youth organizations, non-profits, charities, churches, and other organizing groups to make our information and programs the most accurate and useful resources available to students in the areas in which we operate.
  6. We want to show students that they have the power to enact change–even if they can’t vote.

WE KNOW

  1. We know that many students care deeply about human rights.
  2. We know that students desire to contribute to their communities in a plethora of non-fiscal manners.
  3. We know that students are often limited by availability, conflicting obligations, academic pressures, and lack of information about opportunities.
  4. We know that students often struggle to reach out and develop their own opportunities to contribute societally.
  5. We know that with assistance, information, and community support, any student can find meaningful ways to volunteer and participate in activism.

About

Student Activists For America is a political activism organization for Bay Area students working on issues in today’s America
